abstract: The Covid-19 pandemic has been one of the most devastating events in recent
  history, claiming the lives of more than 5 million people worldwide. Even with the
  worldwide distribution of vaccines, there is an apparent need for affordable, reliable,
  and accessible screening techniques to serve parts of the World that do not have
  access to Western medicine. Artificial Intelligence can provide a solution utilizing
  cough sounds as a primary screening mode for COVID-19 diagnosis. This paper presents
  multiple models that have achieved relatively respectable performance on the largest
  evaluation dataset currently presented in academic literature. Through investigation
  of a self-supervised learning model (Area under the ROC curve, AUC = 0.807) and
  a convolutional nerual network (CNN) model (AUC = 0.802), we observe the possibility
  of model bias with limited datasets. Moreover, we observe that performance increases
  with training data size, showing the need for the worldwide collection of data to
  help combat the Covid-19 pandemic with non-traditional means.
